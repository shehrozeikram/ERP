# Manual Test Guide - Flow 2 (Payment in Advance)

This guide follows the **“Payment in advance”** procurement accounting sequence (your reference sheet):

1. PO generated and approved (no GL in the printed workflow)
2. **Payment by Finance** (vendor advance before GRN)
3. **Material received (GRN)** — inventory in, GRNI
4. **Bill generated by Procurement** — GRNI cleared to AP
5. **Bill payment / adjustment by Finance** — apply party advance; bank payment **zero or partial**
6. **Inventory issuance (SIN)** — project cost / COGS vs inventory

Use it to verify postings and reports end-to-end in this ERP.

---

## A) Preconditions

- Transactional test data cleaned or use a dedicated test vendor and item names.
- Accounts exist and are active (adjust numbers to your chart if different):
  - Inventory (e.g. `1200` Raw Materials — **do not use `1100` if it is AR in your environment**)
  - GRNI (e.g. `2140` or `2100`)
  - `2001` Accounts Payable
  - `5000` COGS (project cost on issue)
  - `1001` Cash / `1002` Bank
  - **Vendor advance:** `1110` Advance to suppliers (this ERP records prepayment here; see note below)
- At least one active project, store, and supplier.

### Note on step 2 vs printed “DR A/P Vendor”

Your paper model shows **DR Accounts Payable / CR Bank** at prepayment. In **this application**, prepayment is booked as **DR `1110` Advance to suppliers / CR Bank (or Cash)** until the vendor bill exists. When you **apply advance** to the bill, the system clears advance against AP so the net effect matches “party advance for adjustment; bank zero or partial” on settlement.

---

## B) Create Inventory Item (Qty = 0)

`Procurement -> Inventory -> New`

- Name / code: e.g. `RM Manual Flow2`
- Opening qty: `0`
- Map **Inventory**, **GRNI**, and **COGS** accounts on the item (same discipline as Flow 1).

Save.

---

## C) Procurement up to Approved PO

1. **Indent** — include your Flow 2 item and quantity (e.g. `100`).
2. **Quotation** — vendor, prices, finalize as per workflow.
3. **PO** — create from quotation; complete **audit / CEO / finance** approvals so the PO is approved.

**Expected:** Operational PO ready; **no requirement** for an AP bill at this stage in the current product flow.

---

## D) Step 2 — Vendor Advance (Finance, before GRN)

`Finance -> Vendors -> Vendor Advance` (URL: `/finance/vendor-advance`)

You can also open **Vendor advance** from the **Accounts Payable** header quick action.

- Select vendor (same as on the PO).
- Amount: e.g. partial or full expected settlement (your sheet uses one amount for the whole story).
- Payment method: Bank (or Cash).
- Optional: **Link to PO** (dropdown loads POs for the selected vendor).
- Reference / narration (or leave blank to auto-generate).
- **Record vendor advance**.

**Expected postings (this ERP):**

- **DR** `1110` Advance to suppliers  
- **CR** `1002` Bank or `1001` Cash  

**Check:** Journal search by payment reference; trial balance still balanced.

---

## E) Step 3 — GRN (Full Receipt)

`Procurement -> Goods Receive`

- Receive against the PO; **full** quantity; unit price aligned with PO; status **Complete** / **Received**.

**Expected postings:**

- **DR** Inventory (item’s inventory account)  
- **CR** GRNI (`2140` / `2100` per mapping)  

**Operational:** Stock quantity and WAC update.

---

## F) Step 4 — Vendor Bill (Procurement)

`Procurement -> Vendor Bills` (create bill from GRN)

- Choose the same vendor.
- Select the GRN just received; create bill for the billable amount; save.

**Expected postings:**

- **DR** GRNI  
- **CR** `2001` Accounts Payable  

**AP:** Bill appears with outstanding = bill amount (before advance application).

---

## G) Step 5 — Apply Advance and Pay Remainder (Finance)

`Finance -> Vendors -> Vendor Bills` (same as Accounts Payable list)

- Open the bill created from the GRN → **View**.
- For **GRN-based** vendor bills, the dialog includes **Payment & advance**: total, advance applied, bank paid, outstanding, **Apply advance to bill**, and **Record bank/cash payment** when balance remains.

**Expected:**

- Advance balance on vendor reduces; bill shows **advance applied**.
- **Bank/Cash payment** for this bill may be **zero** if advance covers the full amount, or **partial** if the bill exceeds the advance (matches your note: *“payment from bank will be zero or partial”*).

If a balance remains, use **Pay bill** for the residual with Bank/Cash.

**Expected additional postings (typical pattern):**

- Application of advance clears **1110** against **2001** (implementation detail in Journal Entries / GL).
- Any residual payment: **DR** `2001` / **CR** Bank or Cash.

**Pass criteria:** Bill fully settled; no unexplained outstanding.

---

## H) Step 6 — SIN (Inventory Issuance)

`Procurement -> Goods Issue`

- Issue a subset of received qty (e.g. `10` of `100`).

**Expected postings:**

- **DR** `5000` COGS (project cost)  
- **CR** Inventory (same account as GRN inventory)  

**Operational:** On-hand qty decreases; WAC unchanged for single-cost receipt.

---

## I) What to Check in Finance (Screen-by-Screen)

### 1) Journal Entries

Search by: advance reference, GRN number, vendor bill number, payment reference, SIN number.

You should see balanced entries for:

- Vendor advance (1110 / bank)
- GRN (inventory / GRNI)
- Bill (GRNI / AP)
- Advance application (+ optional bank payment)
- SIN (COGS / inventory)

### 2) General Ledger

- **1110:** debit at advance; credits when applied to bills  
- **GRNI:** credit at GRN, debit at bill  
- **2001:** credit at bill; debits at apply-advance and payment legs as posted  
- **Inventory / COGS:** same pattern as Flow 1 for GRN and SIN  

### 3) Trial Balance & Balance Sheet

- TB balanced after each major step if you checkpoint.  
- BS: inventory, bank, AP, advance, GRNI should reflect the correct stage (GRNI normally cleared after bill).

### 4) Profit & Loss

- COGS moves when SIN posts (if no sales in the test, P&L may show expense only from issuance, depending on configuration).

---

## J) Quick Pass/Fail Checklist

- [ ] PO approved before advance and GRN  
- [ ] Advance posted: DR 1110 / CR bank (not generic “wrong account”)  
- [ ] GRN: DR inventory / CR GRNI; stock correct  
- [ ] Vendor bill from GRN: DR GRNI / CR AP  
- [ ] Advance applied; bank payment zero or partial as designed; bill settled  
- [ ] SIN: DR COGS / CR inventory  
- [ ] No duplicate SIN COGS journals  
- [ ] TB / BS balanced; GL traceable by reference  

---

## K) Common Mismatch Signals

- **No bill to apply advance to:** Create bill from **Procurement → Vendor Bills** linked to the GRN (bills are not assumed to appear automatically on PO approval in the current flow).  
- **Advance applies but AP still wrong:** Check bill total vs advance amount and whether a second payment was needed.  
- **GRNI wrong account:** PO/GRN line text vs inventory **name** / **itemCode** alignment (same as Flow 1).  

---

## L) Automated regression

After server and DB are up (`E2E_API_URL` if not default), from project root:

```bash
npm run clean:test-data
npm run test:advance-payment-e2e
```

This script exercises indent → PO → **advance payment** → GRN → **vendor bill** → apply advance → optional bank payment → SIN and checks TB/BS/GL/P&L health.
